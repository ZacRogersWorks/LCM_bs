How It Works

On Friday, Oct 13th, local businesses, individuals, and families will gather together with sleeping bags, snacks, flashlights, and… you guessed it, cardboard boxes, to spend a night sleeping on the street to raise money for Lighthouse Christian Ministries to house, feed, and disciple local individuals right here in Wenatchee.

Participants can register here, and pledge to raise a certain amount of money from their friends, family, and peers, which goes straight towards the effort to house, feed, and disciple local homeless individuals.

History

In November of 2011, Shawn Arington, now Lighthouse Director, slept in a box on Wenatchee Ave to raise money and awareness to open the 84 person emergency shelter in Wenatchee. That is, until he was removed by police.

The next year, he was joined by many others (law enforcement included), and the tradition has continued ever since.
Today, there are now over 100 annual participants in Night In A Box.

Here at Lighthouse Christian Ministries, we put an emphasis on Christ’s commission to go to the lost and the least and show them not only love and the Word of God, but also provide them with the support and resources needed to get back on their feet and live a life more abundantly.
